Overview

Priyam Raj is a serial founder and operator based in Bangalore, India. A self-taught builder since 2012, he started by making films and experimenting with hardware projects before transitioning to software. By age 14, he was already earning money online through blogging. He has built and exited two internet companies, and currently operates a venture studio focused on creator and media software. His work has been covered by outlets including The Quint and New York Weekly.

Early life and education

Priyam's family lived in Purnia, Bihar until 2012. One of four siblings, he grew up as a maker and creator, spending his childhood building remote-controlled airplanes, skateboarding, and making short films. These early creative pursuits shaped his approach to problem-solving and product building.

In 2012, when his family moved to Deoghar, Jharkhand, Priyam began teaching himself to code using his brother's laptop. He started experimenting with web projects and making short films for YouTube.

By 2016, at age 14, he had started freelance Android development, publishing 5+ apps to the Play Store that collectively reached over 20,000 downloads. Around the same time, he began blogging about gaming and tech, publishing over 300 articles across multiple blogs that reached 1 million+ views with 95% organic traffic. This work generated $1,000-$1,500 per month — significant income for a teenager — and fueled his drive to keep building online businesses.

In 2017, he founded GameStuff, a gaming blog focused on SEO, which he ran until 2019. In 2019, he launched EzURLs, a site that notified 800+ users daily about free game deals, optimizing it to 60% organic traffic. He ran EzURLs until 2022.

During COVID-19 in 2020, he co-founded Project Multiverse, a content studio producing long-form videos and podcasts that grew to 9,000 subscribers on YouTube. He completed his schooling at DAV Public School in Deoghar.

In 2020, he enrolled at BIT Mesra for a B.Tech in Computer Science. During college, he worked as a software engineer intern at Aamon (Sep 2022 - Mar 2023) and as a machine learning engineer intern at MachineX in Bangalore (May-Jun 2023). He also continued building internet products, including launching Timenite.com in 2019, which he ran until its acquisition in 2023.

In 2023, after his MachineX internship, Priyam moved to Bangalore and worked as a software engineer at CenterPoint Gaming (remote, based in Windsor, Ontario) from October 2023 through November 2024, building internal tools and contributing to their Windows-based electron app. He graduated from BIT Mesra in 2024 and has remained in Bangalore since, focusing on building internet startups.

Career

Exits

  • Timenite.com (Exit #1, Oct 2019 - Aug 2023) — A simple static site that showed countdown to the next Fortnite season. Went viral when streamers began embedding it in their streams. Acquired by CenterPoint Gaming in August 2023. Priyam subsequently worked for CenterPoint Gaming as a software engineer from October 2023 through November 2024.
  • Morise.ai (Exit #2, Jan 2023 - Mar 2024) — An AI-powered YouTube workflow tool. Bootstrapped to ~$550K ARR, achieved #1 Product of the Day on GrowthSchool and #3 Product of the Day on Product Hunt (February 2023). Acquired by Matt Par in March 2024 and rebranded as TubeMagic. The acquisition was featured on Times Square via Acquire.com.

Current ventures

  • TubeMagic (Jan 2024 - Present) — Chief Technology Officer, maintaining and scaling the product post-acquisition.
  • Priyam Ventures Private Limited (Jul 2024 - Present) — Managing Director of his venture studio.
  • Vid.AI (Oct 2024 - Present) — Co-founded with Matt Par. A generative video platform for social-first content creation. Scaled to $1M+ ARR.
  • Warplabs (Jan 2025 - Present) — Founded his software studio and team, based in Bangalore.
  • moonn (Jul 2026 - Present) — Co-founded a payments and community platform based in Ireland.
