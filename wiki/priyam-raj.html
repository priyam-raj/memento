Overview

Priyam Raj is a serial founder and operator based in Bangalore, India. A self-taught builder since 2012, he started by making films and experimenting with hardware projects before transitioning to software. He has built and exited two internet companies, and currently operates a venture studio focused on creator and media software. His work has been covered by outlets including The Quint and New York Weekly.

Early life and education

Born on March 23, 2002, in Purnia, Bihar, Priyam grew up as a maker and creator. His childhood was spent building remote-controlled airplanes, skateboarding, and making short films. These early creative pursuits shaped his approach to problem-solving and product building.

In 2012, when his family moved to Deoghar, Jharkhand, Priyam began teaching himself to code using his brother's laptop. He started experimenting with web projects and grew a YouTube channel where he documented his creative work, eventually reaching over 18,000 subscribers. He completed his schooling at DAV Public School in Deoghar.

In 2020, he enrolled at BIT Mesra for a B.Tech in Computer Science. During college, he built iOS apps and continued developing internet products, refining his skills in product design, software development, and growth.

In 2023, Priyam moved to Bangalore for a summer internship at Listed Inc., where he helped build openinapp.com. He graduated from BIT Mesra in 2024 and has remained in Bangalore since, focusing on building internet startups.

Career

Exits

  • Morise.ai — An AI-powered YouTube workflow tool. Bootstrapped to ~$55K ARR, then acquired by Matt Par and rebranded as TubeMagic. The acquisition was featured on Times Square via Acquire.com.
  • timenite.com — A simple static site that showed countdown to the next Fortnite season. Went viral when streamers began embedding it in their streams.

Current ventures

  • Vid.AI — Co-founded with Matt Par. A generative video platform for social-first content creation. Reached ~$900K ARR within months of launch and valued at $7M as of early 2025.
  • TubeMagic — Maintains the product post-acquisition as CTO.
  • moonn — A payments and community platform, similar to SKWL/School.
  • Warplabs — His software studio and team, based in Bangalore.
